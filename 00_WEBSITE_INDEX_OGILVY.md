## 🎯 OGILVY PRINCIPLES APPLIED THROUGHOUT

### 1. Positioning
- **What does it do?** Supply industrial tools, equipment, and expertise
- **Who is it for?** Shipping companies, manufacturing plants, repair shops
- **Why is it different?** 46 years of experience, real experts (not salespeople), exact inventory, long-term thinking

### 2. The Promise
- Each page has ONE primary promise
- Homepage: "You get reliable supplier who understands your operation"
- Services: "We solve your operational problems, not just sell stuff"
- Brands: "Tools that don't fail when you need them"

### 3. Headlines with Power
- Include brand name (Kolleris)
- Include promise (reliability, expertise, solutions)
- Include news (46 years, specific problems solved)
- Avoid generic phrases ("innovative solutions," "premium quality")

### 4. Problem-First Format
- Every major section starts with customer problem
- Then offers Kolleris solution
- Then explains what customer gets
- Creates rhythm of recognition → relief → confidence

### 5. Specific Facts Over Adjectives
- ❌ Avoid: "extensive experience," "high-quality tools," "innovative solutions"
- ✅ Use: "46 years," "15+ years per specialist," "Milwaukee, Bahco, Knipex"
- ✅ Use: "20-30% reduction in inventory costs," "1-2 day delivery," "5 different suppliers becomes 1"

### 6. Human Tone
- Write like a knowledgeable person, not a corporation
- Use contractions (we're, don't, that's)
- Use "you" language (what you get, your operation)
- Remove corporate jargon ("comprehensive solutions," "synergies," "ecosystem")
